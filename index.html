Life before development

I have a Masters of Communication Sciences and Disorders and was a practicing speech-language pathologist for 5 years before making the transition to development. While my title contains the word "speech", I spent most of my career focusing on disorders of eating and drinking -- the same muscles you use to talk with are the same you use to swallow. And when those muscles don't work properly, what you're eating and drinking can end up in your lungs, which is not good.

I spent over three of my five years at Duke University Hospital and worked very closely with the Cystic Fibrosis population after transplant. These patients are all amazing in their bravery and strength and will remain very special to me.
One of my favorite things to do as a speech pathologist was a swallowing-specific study called a Fiberoptic Endoscopic Evaluation of Swallowing. In my five years, I've scoped over 400 noses! In my time at Duke, I also was lucky enough to study swallowing disorders. I presented at Duke's Use It On Monday Presentation Series, lead a community-foucsed academic journal club, and presented a poster at the national conference.

So, why the change?

I get asked that a lot, and the simplest answer is: I'm inspired by what healthcare could be, not what it currently is. The best mechanism for change that  I could see for me personally, was improving healthcare through tech.
